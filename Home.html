Featured Recipes

Sambar

To make Sambar, cook toor dal until soft. In a pot, sauté mustard seeds, cumin, onions, tomatoes, and mixed vegetables. Add sambar powder, turmeric, and salt. Mix in cooked dal, water, and simmer. Adjust seasoning and add tamarind or lemon for tang. Garnish with coriander. Enjoy!

Chicken Biriyani

To make chicken biryani, marinate chicken pieces with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, and salt. In a separate pan, sauté onions, tomatoes, and spices. Parboil rice and layer it with the cooked chicken mixture. Add fried onions, mint leaves, and saffron-infused milk. Seal and cook until rice is tender and chicken is cooked through. Serve hot garnished with coriander leaves and lemon wedges. Adjust spices to taste. Enjoy the aromatic flavors of this classic Indian dish!

Chicken Curry

To prepare chicken curry, heat oil and sauté onions, garlic, and ginger until golden. Add chicken pieces and brown. Stir in tomato paste, curry powder, turmeric, and salt. Pour in coconut milk and simmer until chicken is cooked through. Adjust seasoning as needed. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve hot with rice or naan. This aromatic and flavorful dish is sure to satisfy your cravings!

Paneer Tkka Masala

For paneer tikka masala, marinate paneer cubes in yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, and salt. Thread onto skewers and grill until lightly charred. In a pan, sauté onions, tomatoes, and spices until softened. Add grilled paneer and simmer in a creamy tomato sauce until flavors meld. Finish with a splash of cream and garnish with fresh cilantro. Serve hot with rice or naan for a delicious vegetarian meal bursting with Indian flavors. Adjust spices to taste and enjoy the rich and creamy indulgence of paneer tikka masala!

Samosa

To make samosas, prepare a dough with flour, salt, and water. Rest it while making the filling. For the filling, sauté onions, potatoes, peas, and spices until soft. Cool. Roll out dough, cut into circles, and fill with the mixture. Fold into triangles and seal. Deep-fry until golden brown and crispy. Serve hot with chutney or sauce. Adjust spices to taste. These triangular delights are perfect as appetizers or snacks, bursting with spicy and savory flavors. Enjoy the crispy goodness of homemade samosas!

Chicken Wings

To prepare chicken wings, marinate with salt, pepper, garlic powder, and paprika. Bake in a preheated oven at 400°F (200°C) for 25-30 minutes until golden and crispy. In a separate saucepan, melt butter and mix with hot sauce, honey, and a splash of vinegar. Toss baked wings in the sauce until coated evenly. Garnish with chopped parsley or green onions. Serve hot with celery sticks and ranch or blue cheese dressing. These spicy and tangy wings are perfect for game nights or gatherings. Adjust sauce levels to your preference. Enjoy the finger-licking goodness!

Manchurian

To make Manchurian, mix grated vegetables like cabbage, carrots, and bell peppers with flour, cornstarch, salt, and pepper. Form into small balls and deep-fry until golden brown. In a separate pan, sauté garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Add soy sauce, tomato ketchup, and vinegar. Thicken with cornstarch slurry. Toss fried vegetable balls in the sauce until coated. Garnish with chopped spring onions. Serve hot as an appetizer or with fried rice. Adjust seasoning and spice levels to taste. Enjoy the Indo-Chinese fusion flavors of this delicious Manchurian dish!

Dosa

To make dosa batter, soak rice and urad dal separately for 6 hours. Grind to a smooth paste, then mix together with salt and let ferment overnight. Heat a non-stick pan, pour a ladleful of batter, and spread thinly in a circular motion. Drizzle oil around the edges and cook until golden brown. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar. Optionally, fill with a potato masala filling for masala dosa. Adjust batter consistency for desired thickness. Enjoy the crispy and flavorful South Indian delicacy of dosa for breakfast or any meal!
